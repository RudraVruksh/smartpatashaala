Foundation
Early Learning Curriculum

Word Smart Verbal Linguistic
With Linguistic-verbal intelligence your children gain high ability to organize the mind and vent ideas well and clearly and be able to use those abilities straightforwardly through words to express these thoughts, whether in speaking, writing, or reading.
Music Smart – Recitation Skills
Music Recitals provide an opportunity for you to show your child that you value their involvement in music. Setting aside time in your busy life to attend a recital supports your children and their peers and shows your child that your family values music.
Numeracy -Logic Smart of Recitation & Recognition Skill
Children think mathematically long before they start school, and early math skills are being used by children throughout their activities, experiences and daily routines, whether at school or at home.
General Awareness -Self Smart of Creativity – Comprehension
The general Awareness that your child acquires during the age of 2.5 to 6 years will open different avenues for them. It will also help improve their social skills, emotional skills, reasoning skills and thinking skills to name a few.
Art and Crafts for Creative learning
Art and crafts will foster early emotional development and offer your child opportunity to express themselves creatively.Art and craft activities give kids a sense of achievement and allow them to take pride in their work which builds confidence.Kids get to try new things and also develop their “self-regulation skills”.This helps them develop patience, which all kids need help with
